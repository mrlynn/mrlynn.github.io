## Writing style — blog posts (`content/blog/*.mdx`)

Michael's writing voice is direct, plainspoken, and specific — closer to personal
storytelling than content marketing. When drafting or editing any blog post, avoid
these AI-isms:

1. **No repeating structural templates.** Don't impose the same bolded-label
   skeleton on every section of a listicle (e.g., "**Why I believed it:** /
   **What's actually true:** / **The proof that convinced me:**"). Write flowing
   narrative paragraphs — let each section take its own shape.
2. **No aphoristic mic-drop closers.** Cut tight-antithesis sentences used as a
   paragraph-ending flourish ("A plugin is a passenger seat. Cursor rebuilt the
   car around the driver."). End sections on a real, specific thought, not a
   quotable summary line.
3. **No corporate/marketing phrasing.** Lines like "rebuilt around AI," "unified
   context model," or "a development platform, not just an editing surface"
   don't survive. If it sounds like a product one-pager, rewrite it as something
   a person would actually say.
4. **Don't lean on em dashes as default connective tissue.** Vary sentence joins
   — periods, "and," "so," "though" — instead of stringing clauses together with
   " - " every other sentence.
5. **Avoid over-parallel construction / rule-of-three.** Sentences like "wrong
   about X, wrong about Y, almost wrong about Z" read as generated, not written.
   Let sentences be a little lumpier and less symmetrical.
6. **Ground claims in one specific, slightly imperfect detail** instead of a
   tidy generalization. Concrete beats smooth.

Calibration example for voice: [content/blog/my-last-one.mdx](content/blog/my-last-one.mdx)
